# Introduction {#introduction}

In a vast empire spanning countless worlds across trillions of universes, the demand for habitable planets never ceases.

*The Vassal Game* places you in the role of an **Imperial Overseer**, tasked with guiding a newly uplifted species as they establish civilization on a freshly terraformed world. This planet, once a barren husk, has undergone **millions of years of accelerated development** inside a **time-dilated field**, its **ecosystems designed, its Qi cultivated, and its first sapient inhabitants uplifted**.

Now, as the time-dilation is relaxed and the first settlers prepare to arrive, your mission is clear‚Äî**refine the world‚Äôs Qi, stabilize its ley-lines, cultivate its planetary core, and prepare for large-scale colonization.** Competing against rival sects and factions, you must balance **growth, governance, and spiritual harmony**, fending off **Beast migrations, internal power struggles, and the ambitions of your own people**.

Will you guide your world to prosperity, securing your species‚Äô legacy among the stars, or will it become just another footnote in the Empire‚Äôs endless expansion?

# The Multiverse and Physical Reality {#the-multiverse-and-physical-reality}

The multiverse is a vast, interconnected reality, structured by cosmic ley lines, dimensional layers, and celestial forces. Each universe, realm, and planet exists within this greater framework, shaped by Qi flow, celestial bodies, and fundamental laws of reality.

## The Structure of the Multiverse {#the-structure-of-the-multiverse}

The multiverse is an infinite network of interconnected realities, each with its own physical, spiritual, and metaphysical rules. However, all realms follow the same fundamental Qi flow laws, linking them together through ley lines and cosmic harmonics.

### Layered Realities & Dimensional Boundaries {#layered-realities-&-dimensional-boundaries}

* The multiverse is structured in layers, with each reality existing within a higher or lower framework.  
* Greater realms sit above mortal existence, often home to Celestial Overseers, ancient cultivators, and cosmic entities.  
* Lower realms contain unstable, fragmented dimensions, sometimes remnants of destroyed worlds or collapsed ley lines.

### Natural & Artificial Dimensional Barriers {#natural-&-artificial-dimensional-barriers}

* Some realities are naturally separate due to Qi turbulence or cosmic instability.  
* Others are artificially locked away‚Äîeither by overseers, sects, or forbidden formations.

üå© Example

* A sealed realm might contain an ancient cultivator, locked away due to the threat they pose to balance.  
* Some sects create artificial pocket realms, sustaining them with compressed ley lines.

## Cosmic Realms and Dimensional Layers {#cosmic-realms-and-dimensional-layers}

The multiverse contains many different types of realms, each playing a role in the flow of Qi and cultivation.

### Higher Realms (Celestial Planes) {#higher-realms-(celestial-planes)}

* Exist beyond the reach of most mortal cultivators.  
* Home to Overseers, Divine Beasts, and ancient immortal sects.  
* Some cultivators ascend to these realms upon reaching Tier 35+, though few return.

### Mortal Realms {#mortal-realms}

* The primary habitable dimensions where empires and sects flourish.  
* Controlled by mortal cultivators, kingdoms, and federations.  
* Follow standard laws of physics and Qi flow, but vary in density and elemental affinity.

### Lower Realms & Nether Zones {#lower-realms-&-nether-zones}

* Fractured, unstable realms where Qi has become chaotic.  
* Some were once mortal worlds that collapsed due to failed planetary breakthroughs.  
* Inhabited by corrupted cultivators, rogue spirits, and chaotic Beasts.

üå© Example

* A failed planetary core expansion might cause a world to descend into a lower realm, turning it into a wasteland of corrupted Qi.

## The Role of Celestial Bodies in Qi Flow {#the-role-of-celestial-bodies-in-qi-flow}

Celestial bodies‚Äîstars, planets, moons, and cosmic phenomena‚Äîaffect the distribution and behavior of Qi in the multiverse.

### Stars and Solar Influence {#stars-and-solar-influence}

* Stars serve as Qi amplifiers, radiating elemental energy based on their composition.  
* Sun-aligned cultivators draw from stellar energy for cultivation.  
* The empire engineers entire solar systems, placing planets in ideal Qi-rich zones.

üå© Example:

* A cultivator aligned with fire may gain immense power by training under an active Qi-rich star.

### Planets and Their Qi Cores {#planets-and-their-qi-cores}

* Every stable planet with Qi cultivation potential possesses a living, sentient planetary core.  
* Cores pull in and refine Qi, distributing it through ley lines across the world.  
* A planet‚Äôs tier is determined by the strength of its core, impacting the density of Qi available.  
* A planets class is determined by a combination of its Qi density, world-core personality, and ability to sustain life and cultivation.

#### Planetary Classification {#planetary-classification}

"*Imperial geologists classify planets based on their Qi stability, core condition, and ability to sustain cultivation. While most planets are Shell Worlds or Matryoshka Worlds, others‚Äîsuch as Cracked Worlds or Machine Worlds‚Äîserve specialized functions in the Empire's expansion strategy.*"

##### Common World Types (Empire-Controlled) {#common-world-types-(empire-controlled)}

1. **Shell Worlds** ‚Üí Standard Imperial worlds with a gravity-attuned core.  
2. **Matryoshka Worlds** ‚Üí Advanced multi-layered Shell Worlds.  
3. **Twin Worlds** ‚Üí Paired planets sharing an atmosphere, linked by natural or artificial bridges.  
4. **Ring Worlds** ‚Üí Massive, ring-shaped megastructures encircling a star, sustained by Qi-stabilized gravity formations.  
5. **Non-Standard Gravity Worlds** \- Generally close to the empire standard for size, but with alternate gravity chosen for special use cases. Combat training, specialized alchemical or crafting areas, homes for special needs groups.  
6. **Sky Worlds** ‚Üí Continent to world-sized islands orbiting natural gas giants with accommodating world-cores.  
7. **Fallen Worlds** ‚Üí Planets suffering Qi corruption or ecological collapse, monitored for rehabilitation.  
8. **Coreless Planets** ‚Üí Planets without a world-core, existing in a Qi-thin state.

##### Unstable & Dangerous Worlds {#unstable-&-dangerous-worlds}

* **Cracked Worlds** ‚Üí Partially collapsed tier-up failures with floating or fractured terrain.  
* **Storm Worlds** ‚Üí Planets locked in perpetual elemental storms, hazardous but valuable.  
* **Qi-Volcanic Worlds** ‚Üí Planets with extreme Qi eruptions, rich in rare materials but deadly to unprotected cultivators.

##### Rare & Unique Worlds {#rare-&-unique-worlds}

* **Rogue World Tree Worlds** ‚Üí Planets drifting through space, carried by massive, sentient world trees.  
* **Void Sanctuaries** ‚Üí Planets or other suitable habitats anchored to a ley-line intersection far enough from anything else to have (almost) only pure and void Qi.  
* **Machine Worlds** ‚Üí Planets where Qi and technology have merged, creating cybernetic or rune-powered civilizations.

### Planetary Features & Megastructures {#planetary-features-&-megastructures}

These natural and artificial planetary features will define civilization, cultivation sites, and exploration zones.

#### Qi-Well Megacities {#qi-well-megacities}

Massive urban centers built directly over powerful ley-line intersections or Qi wells.

* Extreme Qi density makes cultivation vastly more efficient, attracting sect headquarters and research institutions.  
* Hundreds of millions of people may live in these cities, both mortals benefiting from Qi spillover and cultivators seeking the best training environments.  
* The wealthiest sects or noble clans claim prime real estate closest to the core Qi wells.  
  **Use Cases:**  
* Tiered city designs ‚Üí Lower-tier residents live further from the Qi well, while higher-tier cultivators occupy the central zones.  
* Massive rune formations and spirit arrays regulate Qi flow, ensuring safe energy levels for mortals while enhancing high-tier breakthroughs.  
  **Who Watches Them?**  
* The Ministry of Planetary Harmonization (MPH) regulates Qi density to prevent imbalances or excess mutations in mortal populations.  
* Some cultivators fight over control of high-Qi districts, leading to political disputes.

üå© **Example:**  
 *"The Imperial City of Shen-Lan is built atop a Qi-well so vast that sect palaces float kilometers above the ground on suspended spirit formations. Only those who have earned their place in the city's hierarchy are allowed into the highest sanctuaries."*

#### Floating Islands & Sky Sanctuaries {#floating-islands-&-sky-sanctuaries}

Landmasses suspended above the surface, either by Wind Qi anomalies, naturally forming levitation fields, or artificial Qi formations.

* Prime locations for isolated cultivation retreats and sect strongholds.  
* Some islands drift naturally, while others are anchored using spiritual formations.  
* Many floating islands never touch the surface, forming hidden aerial civilizations.  
  **Use Cases:**  
* Rare herbs and Beasts evolve differently, making floating islands prime alchemy and resource sites.  
* Some islands are inhabited by aerial Beasts, complicating settlements but offering rare cultivation opportunities.  
  **Who Watches Them?**  
* Independent sects often claim floating islands as personal training zones.  
* The Imperial Navigation Bureau monitors major island clusters to prevent collisions with sky cities or trade routes.

üå© **Example:**  
 *"A sect fortress rests upon a floating mountain range, suspended by Wind Qi. It has not touched the ground in millennia, and its disciples learn to fly before they can walk."*

#### Natural Qi Rings {#natural-qi-rings}

Unlike engineered orbital rings, these naturally occurring bands of Qi orbit planets like glowing celestial halos.

* Qi-rich environments ideal for cultivation, but few permanent settlements due to unstable conditions.  
* Extreme-speed travel possible by riding the Qi current, used by high-tier sect messengers.  
* Opposing Qi Rings (one moving clockwise, one counter-clockwise) generate immense turbulence, making travel dangerous but rewarding.  
  **Use Cases:**  
* Floating islands and artificial Qi-stabilized habitats anchor themselves in rings.  
* Qi Rings often shift or break, triggering massive storms, Beast migrations, or temporary rift phenomena.  
  **Who Watches Them?**  
* The Ministry of Spatial Order (MSO) maps Qi Rings and predicts their movement patterns.  
* Some independent nomadic sects live within Qi Rings, following their cycles.

üå© **Example:**  
 *"The Twin Qi Rings of Xian-Wu orbit in opposite directions, creating a turbulent energy vortex. Some cultivators ride the shifting currents, developing flight techniques that are impossible elsewhere."*

#### Rift Zones & Dungeons {#rift-zones-&-dungeons}

Naturally occurring or stabilized pocket dimensions, forming self-contained sub-realities accessible via rift gates.

* Some are stable, remaining open for centuries, while others fluctuate unpredictably.  
* Some have temporal distortions, where time moves faster or slower than the outside world.  
* Natural rifts exist outside formations, while others are anchored and monitored by the MSO.  
  **Use Cases:**  
* Rift exploration is a high-risk, high-reward endeavor, offering Qi-rich artifacts, lost techniques, and materials from other universes.  
* Some rifts connect to failed worlds, allowing exploration of ruins and fragmented histories.  
  **Who Watches Them?**  
* The MSO regulates known stable rifts but cannot control wild rift generation.  
* Some rogue factions exploit rifts, making smuggling and criminal operations difficult to police.

üå© **Example:**  
 *"A rift appears once every century, connecting a mortal realm to a fragment of a destroyed celestial battlefield. Artifacts found there still hold traces of divine Qi, but only the strongest dare enter."*

#### Sleeping Titans (Cultivator & Beast Fossil Worlds) {#sleeping-titans-(cultivator-&-beast-fossil-worlds)}

Ancient cultivators or Beasts that have remained in deep meditation for so long that geological processes have integrated them into the landscape.

* Entire civilizations can live on their backs without realizing their true nature.  
* Some Titans are still alive, occasionally shifting, causing massive quakes or natural disasters.  
  **Use Cases:**  
* Prime locations for Qi absorption and artifact retrieval.  
* Legends speak of those who awaken a Titan, either gaining an eternal master or unleashing an unstoppable force.  
  **Who Watches Them?**  
* The Empire tracks known Titan locations but does not interfere unless one awakens.  
* Sects may set up strongholds, believing they can one day inherit the Titan‚Äôs power.

üå© **Example:**
